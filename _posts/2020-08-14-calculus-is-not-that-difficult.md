When people hear the dreaded *"Calculus"* mentioned in conversation, it is quickly objectified as some sort of insanely complicated subject that you need a PhD at the end of your name to grasp. Often you will hear people saying that they *"aren't smart enough"* for spooky scary Calculus. Once you start taking a Calculus I class however, the fuzzy concepts like "area under a curve" and "instantaneous slope" become understood, and you begin to wonder why the subject has such a reputation for being impossibly hard.

To a non-math reader, this mindset change might come off as a weird humble brag or something, however this idea can be applied to any topic or skill under the horizon. The general thought process tends to go as follows: 

<ol type="I">
<li>You see some complicated skill being performed or explained</li>
<li>You have little experience in learning complicated skills</li>
<li>"I'm too dumb to ever understand xyz"</li>
</ol>
