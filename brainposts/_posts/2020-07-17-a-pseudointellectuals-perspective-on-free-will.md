Assuming free will is nonexistant, our brains are directed by the sensations they feel, whether they be feelings of security, satisfaction, contentment, pleasure, etc... Free will quickly becomes a very scary thing to think about, because if it is merely an illusion, we are essentially watching a very terrifying and immersive movie without truly being in control of what is happening. 

Maybe I'm just having a disconnect from reality, but really, what the hell is this human condition thing I'm experiencing? All of the feelings, the high emotions. the low emotions, all of it is surreal to think about. What even is the flow of time? We're born, and then we die, and then in the middle there's this crazy mess called life. People who don't have the capacity to truly philosophize about the absurdness of it all seem to be totally content with it. I feel as though the higher you go on the intelligence ladder, the more capable you are of contracting depression from thinking too hard about the future, the past, the present, everything and anything, etc... \*Afternote: this correlates a lot more with neuroticism than anything else, but the point still stands.

# Quick Tangent on the Emotional Aspect of Music

Going back to the whole chasing feelings concept, I think music plays a deep and meaningful role in it all. Expressing emotions through music is much easier than writing a poem or something, because music captures all of the subtlties of emotion that simply cannot be expressed with language alone. "But uhh... music is just words silly" I would have to disagree, as music is the whole experience, not just the lyrics. Plus, have you heard of <a target="_blank" href="https://youtu.be/8GW6sLrK40k">Resonance?</a>

A direct line between human emotion and music would be the fact that the vast majority of songs are written about love and relationships. What are humans hardwired to do? Create relationships with people to facilitate reproduction and longevity of offspring. This underlying motor powering the entire human race has been manifested in the ratio of emotional music to non-emotional music (that is, sexual and romantic emotions). Although brains are complex to the point of us having to resort to shortcuts in order to understand them, our barebones motherboards are the same as any other being in existance.

# It's Autopilot Tunnel Vision Bunny Rabbits All The Way Down

I have this weird theory of "autopilot tunnel vision bunny rabbit" mode being the foundation of living creatures, in relation to everything chasing feelings. I believe that I experienced this "mode" most deeply when I hit the low part of my psychedelic trip. "ATB" consists of essentially letting your feelings control everything you do via a super low intelligence state. The bunny rabbit part comes from the way rabbits seem to be soulless little robots of creatures. They really do just be hopping themselves around on total autopilot in nature, being hypersensitive to external stimuli like predators and sex (hense the saying "fucking like rabbits"). 

The question is: "Do soulless bunny rabbits have free will?" You might say that we as humans have the ability to have done otherwise because of how developed our brains are, but really when you think about it, rabbits and humans are both creatures with similar motivations (sex, food, not dying, etc...). It would be contradictory to suggest that rabbits don't have free will, but we do. The reason we are even able to contemplate the concept of free will at such an intellectual level is because of our deeply complex and highly developed brains. As a result, there is an illusion of free will created by our ability to think about things deeper than what a rabbit would think about. Stoicism is a direct antithesis to this concept of ATB state, so I believe there is a good chance that I am actually on to something with this theory.
