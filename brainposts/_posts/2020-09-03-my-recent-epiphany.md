As far as personal boundaries go, I do think there are some things in my life that I should keep to myself. However, I feel as though there are some people who could benefit from my point of view in the realm of mental health. I've recently taken another dive into the fantastic world of dissociation, which has strangely benefited my understanding of my upbringing and my current state. I figured that I should write a paragraph or two on my observations in the hopes of helping anyone else who relates to my circumstances.

In my second blog post, titled _"A Pseudointellectual's Perspective on Free Will"_ (aptly named), I discussed some (not all) of the ideas I acquired after tripping on psychedelic shrooms. One of the topics of the post was this idea of _"autopilot tunnel vision bunny rabbit"_ mode, which consists of constantly chasing things that bring you to the comfort zone / make you feel good. What I failed to realize, however, was that this feeling has actually manifested in me as a result of personal issues, and that my psychedelic trip was what brought it to the surface.

So like, obviously I don't reeeeally feel like disclosing everything that I've experienced in childhood, but to make a deep story shallow, it kinda sucked a bunch. I match every single symptom of CEN to a tea, and it's basically the primary source of my suffering. In conjunction with being an introverted thinker -type person, my brain is basically a big bowl of depressing spaghetti at the moment. 

I have decided to take a different type of psychedelic drug every other month in an attempt to self-treat and intellectualize my issues (pro-tip: try to avoid that second one whenever possible), but I also plan on acquiring a therapist in addition to this. My last counselor did a garbage job of diagnosing my issues, which I guess was sorta warranted because I myself didn't yet understand the source of my suffering yet. I just figured _"oh, I can't be in a relationship because I'm sOcIaLLy aWkwArd"_ but I really just have emotional issues.

You might be wondering now: _"now aren't you going to write some advice to help out other people in a similar situation???"_ At the moment, I can't totally come up with a complete thought without it turning into uuugggghhh, so instead I plan on understanding the variables of the situation before trying to explain it further. After having a mental breakdown while having my mother stare at me with cold emotionless eyes and my very mAsCuLiNe ( jk ) father sitting on his recliner, I decided that recovering from childhood would be something I would invest a lot of energy into, instead of hoping I just _"grow out of it"_ or whatever.

# Afternotes

I still want to make internet videos talking about my perspective on life, but I'm gonna hold off until I'm less of an anxious mess (probs late sept. / early oct. for the first real upload). I realize that I don't really have an audience, but that isn't at all the point of me starting a channel. I want to have some way to document my current state as well as my progress with developing myself, and making videos seems like the best way to do it (blog posts aren't really the same tbh). I really want to stay away from becoming a one-dimensional boring programmer nerd, so don't expect any _"Here's how to write a wEbScRaPer owowo"_ videos until I develop myself a bit more. Thanks for reading this, whoever you are!
