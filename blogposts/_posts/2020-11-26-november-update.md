Hello all! It has been awhile since I last posted on this site, which considering the circumstances of the world is not at all surprising. I said I planned to start up a youtube channel but surprise surprise!! I didn't really get around to it. I do have some outlines of ideas I have in mind, but I'm basically hanging by a thread in my mental space so creating videos lies low on my priority list lmao. *(I'll get around to it eventually, just not until later)* Luckily, education-wise I'm not doing too bad. Although my high standards are telling me that I could be getting a 4.0 right now, I think that my current grades could be much worse than they currently are.

Anyways, as far as the direction for this blog is concerned, I do wish to get back into expressing myself through these posts. Eventually, I would like to upgrade from sob stories to actual content worth giving up your time to read. Unfortunately, it might be a hot couple years before I'll be able to provide that to my readers *(of which there are precisely zero, to be clear)*, as my spiralling transition into adulthood is overwhelming me with things to work on and to think about. 

## More sad stuff that won't be read by anyone for a long time:

For instance, my level of anxiety has gotten to be a bit much to handle, which when combined with academic circumstances *(zoooom!!)* has left me feeling quite lonely. As time has progressed further, my fun brain that was filled with constant pondering and curiosity has slowly become more and more jaded at reality, and my brain has very noticebly stopped operating in its usual constantly thinking manner, and instead has filled with a literal void of nothingness. In many ways, feeling nothing is worse than feeling sad, because at least feeling sad lets you feel your emotions and allows you to sort through them. 

But hey, that's just me being nonspecific about my life that avoids anyone who actually reads this from getting to know the real me, which is what I also happen to see other people who have a bunch of headmess do; "Look at me I'm sad, weed drugs instability no life goals wahhh!!". Except the thing is, I've purposely tried to avoid paths like drug/alcohol addiction and I have crafted myself a vague career goal that is maybe possibly certainly going to place me in a better place once I get through school. Yet still, I feel as though I'm constantly trying to swim upwards to prevent myself from drowning in my head.

## Looking up though!

Hopefully, I see myself sorting my way through life's fun mental garbage as I progress in my career and gain some financial stability. In the next year or two, I'll land a not-depressing-as-fuck job that will get me enough money to be able to afford the schooling that will *\*probably\** move me up and out of my spaghetti goo state. 

> Seriously though, I've worked at literal fucking McDonalds @$10/hr (partly because my friend worked there, partly for the meme), an Amazon warehouse (chronic hopelessness in exchange for $15 an hour, which two of my other friends also just so happened to take up at the same time as me for *some* reason), and am now currently at a nursing home feeding depressed old people food @$12/hr. It's hard to tell at this point if all my jobs are depressing, or if I'm the one who makes them so (probs a bit of both). Washtenaw Community College's Barnes and Nobel has open applications at ~$2 less than the nursing home, and I'm thinking about taking them up just to give me some mental stability.

Learning the R programming language is next on my list of things to fill my void with *(and also to give me a head start in my later statistics classes lol)*. I've recently gotten into modding Thinkpads with souldering irons and shit, which once I get better at it should be a really rewarding hobby. I've learned to touchtype at a super basic level, and with a bit more practice on [typing.com](https://www.typing.com/student/lessons) I can get up to a proficient speed. 

My plan to get an internsip this summer is likely not going to happen, but if I'm lucky I might be able to get myself a temp IT Technician position or whatever if I feel like crippling myself for some masochistic reason. *If my parents understood the concept of a education savings account and/or the idea of actually going to a university to increase future earning potential and overall higher levels of stability, I wouldn't be struggling as much,* but playing with my hand of cards is gonna have to work for now.
